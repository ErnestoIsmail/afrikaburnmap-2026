Page 31 (Medics)
MEDICS
Real trauma only, please. The desert doesn't have a reset button, and our medics aren't here to tuck you in after a tequila-induced poor decision. We have a world-class field hospital at Off-Centre Camp (OCC) (look for the Big Red Cross).
Hangovers, hangnails, and hol-pyn are not emergencies. Practice Radical Self-Reliance. Pack a proper First Aid Kit for the small stuff and take your chronic meds as prescribed.
PS: Absolutely NO IV bars or rogue 'camp doctor' services.

THE SANCTUARY
Sometimes the prism refracts a little too much and things get overwhelming. The Sanctuary is a safe space for anyone who finds themselves a bit over the edge, or emotionally a bit gefok.

Page 31 (Support: Mental Health and Crisis Care / The Sanctuary)
SUPPORT: MENTAL HEALTH AND CRISIS CARE
Support is a team of experienced mental health professionals providing 24/7 crisis care for mental health or emotional emergencies in the dust.
If you or someone you know needs urgent help, ask the Sanctuary shift lead to contact us. If you spot an emergency out in the field, ask Rangers or Guardians to make contact and we'll be dispatched to you.

The Sanctuary: A Safe Space
You can find us at OCC on Binnekring Road between Medics and Rangers. Our blue-and-white sign is visible day and night.
The Sanctuary is a safe, supportive space for anyone who needs a break from overwhelm or some quiet.
Volunteers offer peer support for challenging psychedelic or emotional experiences, with rest, refreshment, and company available inside or around the fire.
Guests can stay for a few hours, after which campmates can help them return to camp.
The Sanctuary is not an alternative sleeping space if your camp is noisy or annoying. You are welcome while you need us, but it's not a hotel.

Page 34 (Rangers: Through The Prism And Into The Dust)
RANGERS: THROUGH THE PRISM AND INTO THE DUST
When things in Tankwa Town go sideways, look for the orange shirts.
Rangers are fellow burners who volunteer to help keep the peace. They listen, step in when situations get a bit heated, and help people figure things out before it turns into a full-blown desert drama.
They're not security and definitely not the polisie, just calm humans in the dust when things get a little complicated.
If you've got patience, empathy, and the ability to keep your cool when the vibes get weird, you might make a good Ranger.
It takes training, though - helping sort a desert dispute or steady someone who's lost their gees isn't something you just wing.
Swing past Ranger HQ, the bright orange container on the Binnekring near 6ish (you can't miss it), and say hello to the crew.
If you're keen to step up, training runs at 9 am on: Monday, 27 April / Tuesday, 28 April / Wednesday, 29 April
Come learn the ropes and help keep the city a little bit safer and a lot more lekker.

DIE YSKAS
The only place in Tankwa Town where 'commerce' (for ice only!) happens, and keeping that fridge running is a monumental job.
From managing the epic Binnekring queue to tossing 5 kg bags into the arms of overheating burners, Die Yskas is the frontline of desert survival.

Page 30 (DIE YSKAS)
DIE YSKAS
In the only city where money is of no use to you, Die Yskas is the one exception. It's the only place in Tankwa Town where 'commerce' actually happens, and for good reason. Lukewarm gin and vrot boerewors are the enemies of a good Burn.

The Cold Hard Facts
When: Open daily from 11 am to 1 pm, Monday to Saturday.
The Damage: Ice is R100 per 5kg bag.
The Coordinates: The entrance is at Off-Centre Camp on the Binnekring. Follow the sound of rattling coins and the sight of melting dreams.

Ice Etiquette
Park the Wheels: Leave your bike on the rack and your car at your campsite. Navigating the Binnekring in a bakkie just for a bag of cubes is a high-level 'darkwad' move.
The Buddy System: A 5kg bag gets surprisingly heavy when you're trekking through 38°C dust. Bring a friend, a gym bag, a backpack, or a foldable cooler to haul the loot.
Pro Tip: Make friends with the Art Car crew. They are usually fuelled by whimsy and happy to assist with a 'drive-by icing' if you ask nicely.
By Wednesday or Thursday, the crew inside Die Yskas has explained the process approximately 4,000 times. They are sleep-deprived, dusty, and potentially a little snippy. Please keep the vibes chilled and remember that the beasts of Die Yskas are volunteers just like everyone else in Tankwa Town.

Page 35 (Toilets)
TOILETS
Look for the chimneys and lights.
If it didn't come out of your body, it doesn't go in the potty. Our throne toilets are located around the edges of the camping areas and some are in the Binnekring. Use them. No more longdrops or porta potties. Get on your throne and you'll still get a lovely view.
The toilets are kitted with tampon, wet wipes or condoms bins. Use those rather than chucking tampons in the toilets, thanks.
Please put the paper back in the bucket. If you don't, the wind will create toilet snakes (white paper ribbons) all over the Tankwa, which is severely kak for our Leave No Trace (LNT) crew, who prefer real snakes to paper ones.
During the rollout of the event, such as Build Week or early in the event week, some toilets are kept battened shut to reduce pumping, cleaning, maintenance, and smell. Please respect the closed loos and don't open them for general use.
There are also a number of toilets placed in Tankwa Town specifically for disabled participants. These toilets are requested ahead of time by those who need them.
