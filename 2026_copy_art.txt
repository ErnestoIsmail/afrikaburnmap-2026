Please respect the artworks and the artists who worked hard to create them. Unless there's an explicit invitation to interact with a piece, please refrain from tipping, molesting, climbing on, leaning against, or rearranging it.
Also, please don't scrawl 'Free Greenland!' or 'For a good time call Sweet Lollipop' on random artworks, no matter how tempting it might be.

Sign of the Time (SI)
Artist: Nyaniso Lindi
The project is inspired by historical events that took place in Makhanda (Grahamstown). In 1819, a group of Xhosa warriors led by Makhanda Nxele came to attack Grahamstown, and a small group of English soldiers led by Colonel John Graham managed to defeat them. The area where the battle took place is known as Egazini (Place of Blood). The work captures the hill where the Xhosa warriors stood.

Tuning Fork (TF)
Artist: Conrad Hicks
This piece responds to a calling from the land to be healed. Destructive energy, rooted in unresolved trauma, conflict over water access, and historical wounds carried in water, needs to be diffused before it causes further harm. This work listens to that pain and works to prevent it from passing through the land into us. You're invited to consciously, with gratitude and love, infuse water, sprinkle it at the base of the sculpture, then strike the tuning fork. Meditate on acknowledging our ancestors' pain and our responsibility to make repairs for the earth and water, with love.
